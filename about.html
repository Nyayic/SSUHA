Background

South Sudan Health Association (SSUHA) is a National Non-Government Organization (NNGO) registered with the South Sudan Relief and Rehabilitation Commission (RRC) under the new NGOs act with registration number 009 and was founded in 1995 to assist in supporting Sudanese refugees in Kenya and those living in the Internally Displaced Peoples (IDP) camps in Bamurye in Kajo-Keji County. The aim was to meet the health and social needs of the refugees and IDPs.

To date, a number of significant development partners and donors have supported SSUHA to implement several Programs which includes:

  1. OFDA through ARC for Kajo-Keji County.
  2. UNICEF for Kajo-Keji County.
  3. CRS for Kajo-Keji.
  4. Sudan Medical Link through the diocese of Salisbury for Kajo-Keji County.
  5. The European Union (EU) through UNDP in an ICCO-led consortium with 4 other NGOs that included AAH-I, ZOA, NSCC, and SCOPE, for western Juba county payams. 6- UNHCR-IGAD for Kajo-Keji County.
  6. The European Union (EU) through UNDP in a ZOA-led consortium with IPCS for western Juba county payams.
  7. HEKS/Swiss Solidarity for 4 payams in Lainya and Yei counties.
  8. MTDF for Kajo-Keji County.
  9. Jhpiego/MCHIP for Lainya County.
  10. HPF for Lainya county. HEKS/THD for Yei county.
  11. SSJR/TDH for Lainya county.

Background

Settling Refugees in IDP Camps

Health

Coordinate, collaborate and partner with the Ministry of Health (MoH), LGAs and other stakeholders to support health services.

Water & Sanitation

Promote health and hygiene awareness, form and train community -based WASH committees, provide spare parts and tools for rehabilitation of water sources, conduct quality testing to provide safe water, facilitate and promote establishment of water sources, public VIP latrines and household pit latrines.

Sexual & Reporductive Health

Provide ANC and safe motherhood, promote family planning, promote child health, promote adolescent reproductive health, manage abortions, HIV/AIDS/STIs, promote management and prevention of infertility.

HIV & AIDS

Promote VCT and access to ARVs, prevent MTCT, promote home-based care, campaign against co-infections, promote the use of condoms, promote community and stakeholders’ engagement in campaign, promote the use of IEC materials and radio talk shows.

Food security & Livelihoods

Increase and diversify agricultural production, facilitate surplus production, improve natural resource management and sustainable land use option.

Protection

Provide psychosocial support, conduct case management, strengthen community linkages and referral pathways for SGBV, provide women and girls/boys friendly spaces, conduct family tracing and re-unification, provide training and awareness at schools and host communities.

Vision

A sustainable, healthy and productive society living in harmony.

Mission

To improve access to quality and affordable health and other social services to communities in its area service coverage in South Sudan through:

  • Infrastructural and institutional development.
  • Capacity building, educational training and awareness raising.
  • Networking and partnership.
  • Lobbying and advocacy
